Constitutive Models – Parameter Requirements

LIDL LGZ Durres · Porto Romano, Albania · Boti (2025) stratigraphy · For Midas GTS NX & Plaxis 2D/3D

Mohr-Coulomb (MC) Simple

Elastic-perfectly plastic · Available in Midas GTS NX & Plaxis

Six-parameter model: linear elastic until the Mohr-Coulomb yield surface is reached, then perfectly plastic. Does not capture stress-dependent stiffness or hardening. Recommended as a benchmark and for firm/stiff layers (Strata IV, VI). For soft clays, results in poor settlement prediction (overestimates heave on unloading).

Hardening Soil (HS) Advanced

Isotropic hardening (deviatoric + volumetric) · Plaxis / Midas GTS NX

Hyperbolic stress-strain in drained triaxial compression, combined with a Cam-Clay compression cap. Captures stress-dependent stiffness and densification of sands. Recommended for Strata II (loose sand) and V (dense sand). Better than MC for all materials, but requires full TxCD stress-strain curves.

Soft Soil Creep (SSC) Best for soft clays

Modified Cam Clay + viscoplastic creep · Plaxis / Midas GTS NX (Soft Ground)

Extends the Soft Soil model with a third compressibility line (μ*) to capture secondary consolidation (creep). STRONGLY RECOMMENDED for Strata I and III (soft clays under warehouse slab). Without creep, long-term settlements are underestimated. All parameters derivable from the current oedometer programme with additions.
